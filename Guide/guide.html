Intro

Epidemics and pandemics are not going anywhere and will forever be a part of history. Mask-It-Or-Casket was created to help visualize disease spread and the impact of policy implementations on it. Aspects of the game were included to make the game more realistic - money and time is needed for policies, vaccines need to be developed before they can be used, the risk bar was based off a model the New York Times used to assess transmission risk. We hope Mask-It-Or-Casket will give you some insight into some of the work that goes into fighting against epidemics and pandemics!


How to Win

Strategize and implement policies within your budget and in time.
Keep the risk as low as possible so you can gain more green points and reduce your red points.

Long story short: Protect the people from the disease in time to save as many people as you can!

Risk Bar

Green Points

You can earn green points by keeping your risk level low, or in the green section of the risk bar.
Earn green points to win the game!



Red Points

You gain red points when your risk level is high, or in the red section of the risk bar.
You lose if you gain too many red points!

Citizens (Part 1)

The citizens will have one of four base states:

Citizens who have not been infected by the virus will be shown as a plain circle.

Citizens who are infected by the virus will be shown as a green virus.

Citizens who were infected by the virus, but have recovered will be shown as a plain circle with green arms.

Citizens who have died because of the virus will be shown as a skull.

Citizens (Part 2)

If a citizen is following a certain policy, then their base icon will have one of the following effects over their icon.

*If the citizen is following multiple policies, then the policy that better protects them from the virus will be displayed. The policies are listed in order of effectiveness.

Citizens who have been vaccinated will have a blue aura around their base icon.

Citizens who are wearing a mask will be a shown with a mask over their base icon.

Citizens who are social distancing, will have an arrow pointing in both directions over their icon.
